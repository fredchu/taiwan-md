# Wu Ming-yi

Wu Ming-yi stands as contemporary Taiwan's most important nature writing author, a literary master who weaves ecological poetry through words. Born in 1971, he observes the fusion of nature and humanity through a unique compound-eye perspective, creating unprecedented literary landscapes. From insect researcher to literary creator, his works including "The Man with the Compound Eyes" and "The Stolen Bicycle" have not only caused sensations in the Chinese literary world but made him the first Taiwan writer to be longlisted for the International Man Booker Prize. As a professor in the Department of Chinese Literature at National Dong Hwa University, Wu Ming-yi serves not only as creator but as an important advocate for Taiwan's nature writing tradition, using literature to voice ecological conservation and showcase Taiwan literature's unique charm to the world.

## Early Life and Academic Journey

### Taipei Growing Years

Born June 20, 1971, in Taipei, Wu Ming-yi spent his childhood and adolescence in an urban environment. However, unlike many children raised in concrete jungles, Wu harbored deep interest and keen observational powers regarding the natural world from early age.

**Childhood nature enlightenment:**
Despite living in urban surroundings, Wu demonstrated strong interest in insects and small animals during childhood. He frequently observed insects and plants near home—these early natural observation experiences planted important seeds for his later literary creation.

**Family environment influence:**
Wu's family wasn't a literary household, but family members supported and tolerated his interests and hobbies. This free growing environment allowed him to develop according to personal interests without traditional framework constraints.

**Natural experiences on urban edges:**
In Taipei's peripheral areas, Wu found many opportunities to contact nature. These natural spaces within cities later became important scenes and inspiration sources in his literary works.

### University Period Exploration

**Chinese literature studies:**
Wu chose Chinese Literature Department for university, laying deep foundations for his literary creation. During university, he not only studied traditional Chinese literature but also encountered modern literary theory and creative techniques.

**Literary club participation:**
During university, Wu actively participated in various literary club activities. He began attempting creation and published works in campus literary magazines. These early creative experiences significantly influenced his literary style formation.

**Natural science interests:**
Besides literature, Wu maintained strong interest in natural sciences. He frequently read books related to biology and ecology—this interdisciplinary knowledge structure provided scientific foundations for his later nature writing.

**Creative concept germination:**
During university, Wu began contemplating how to combine love for nature with literary creation. He realized Taiwan literature lacked deep nature writing, becoming an important direction for his future creation.

### Graduate School Turning Point

**Chinese literature graduate studies:**
After university graduation, Wu entered Chinese Literature graduate school for further study. During graduate school, he studied literary theory more deeply, particularly modern literary criticism theory.

**Amateur entomology research:**
Beyond formal literary research, Wu conducted amateur entomology research. He spent considerable time observing and recording various insects' ecological habits—this scientific research experience provided unique perspectives for his literary creation.

**Combining creation with academics:**
During graduate school, Wu began contemplating how to combine academic research with literary creation. He realized rigorous academic research methods could bring new possibilities to literary creation.

**Critical decision at 29:**
Wu once set himself a deadline: if he couldn't write successful works by age 30, he would abandon creation. Fortunately, "Moths" was published when he was 29, and this book's success confirmed his creative path.

## "Moths": Beginning of Nature Writing

### Creative Background and Motivation

**Fascination with butterflies:**
"Moths" creation stemmed from Wu's deep fascination with butterflies. He spent years studying Taiwan's butterfly ecology, accumulating vast observational data and profound insights.

**Fusion of science and literature:**
This book's greatest feature was perfectly fusing scientific observation with literary writing. Wu used poetic language to describe butterfly ecological habits, creating entirely new writing modes.

**Mourning for vanishing nature:**
"Moths" represented not only praise for butterflies but mourning for gradually disappearing natural environments. Wu used words to record beautiful lives that might soon vanish.

### Writing Style and Characteristics

**Poeticization of prose:**
Wu demonstrated highly poeticized prose style in "Moths." He used rich metaphors and symbols, elevating butterfly observations to philosophical contemplation.

**Literaturization of scientific spirit:**
The book displayed scientific observation's rigor, but this scientific spirit was wrapped in literaturized language, forming unique aesthetic effects.

**Integration of cross-disciplinary knowledge:**
Wu integrated knowledge from biology, ecology, literature, philosophy, and other fields, creating multi-layered textual meanings.

### Literary Response and Influence

**Taiwan nature writing milestone:**
"Moths" was hailed as an important milestone in Taiwan nature writing. It initiated Taiwan literature's deep attention to natural environments, influencing many later writers.

**Awards and recognition:**
This book won multiple important literary awards and was selected as an annual good book. Its success established Wu's foothold in literary circles.

**Beginning of international attention:**
"Moths" also began receiving international literary attention. Some foreign nature literature researchers noticed this emerging Taiwan writer.

**Creative model establishment:**
This book established Wu's unique creative model: based on scientific observation, using literary techniques for deep writing. This model became the basic characteristic of all his later works.

## "The Man with the Compound Eyes": Ecological Novel Classic

### Creative Background and Conception

**Compound eye metaphor:**
"The Man with the Compound Eyes" title comes from insect compound eye structure. Wu used this metaphor to express the importance of observing the world from multiple perspectives.

**Environmental crisis concern:**
The novel's creative background was deep concern for global environmental crisis. Wu wanted to warn humanity about serious consequences of environmental destruction through fictional stories.

**Combining science fiction with reality:**
"The Man with the Compound Eyes" possesses science fiction qualities, but its foundation is profound understanding of real environmental problems. Wu used imagination to amplify real environmental crises.

### Story Structure and Themes

**Weaving multi-line narrative:**
The novel adopts multi-line narrative structure, skillfully weaving different story lines together. Each line represents different perspectives and positions.

**Indigenous culture integration:**
Wu integrated Taiwan indigenous cultural elements into the novel. He believed indigenous ecological wisdom holds important revelatory significance for modern society.

**Environmental justice exploration:**
The novel explores environmental justice issues. Wu points out that environmental destruction often causes greater harm to disadvantaged groups.

**Future world imagination:**
"The Man with the Compound Eyes" depicts a future world severely damaged by environmental destruction. This imagination carries strong warning significance.

### International Influence and Evaluation

**Translation and overseas publication:**
"The Man with the Compound Eyes" was translated into multiple languages and published in many countries. It introduced international readers to Taiwan's environmental problems and literary creation.

**Environmental literature paradigm:**
This novel was hailed as a paradigmatic environmental literature work. It proved literature can play important roles in environmental protection.

**Academic research subject:**
"The Man with the Compound Eyes" became the subject of much academic research. Researchers analyzed this work from different angles including ecocriticism, postcolonial theory, and science fiction literature.

**International award recognition:**
The novel won the French Islands Literature Prize, representing important international literary recognition of Wu's creative achievements.

## "The Stolen Bicycle": Interweaving Personal and Historical

### International Man Booker Prize Breakthrough

**2018 historic longlisting:**
On March 12, 2018, the International Man Booker Prize announced its longlist, with Wu Ming-yi's "The Stolen Bicycle" listed among them, making him the first Taiwan writer longlisted for this award. This news caused tremendous sensation in Taiwan's literary circles.

**International literary award significance:**
The International Man Booker Prize is hailed as one of the most important literary awards in the English-speaking world. Wu's longlisting represented not only personal honor but an important milestone in Taiwan literature's internationalization.

**Taiwan literature's international visibility:**
This longlisting greatly increased Taiwan literature's international visibility. Many foreign readers and publishers began paying attention to Taiwan's literary creation.

**Sovereignty dispute insistence:**
During the longlisting process, Man Booker Prize organizers once labeled Wu's nationality as "Taiwan, China." Wu seriously protested and insisted on correction to "Taiwan," demonstrating his insistence on Taiwan sovereignty.

### Novel Themes and Structure

**Personal memory and collective history:**
"The Stolen Bicycle" uses a stolen bicycle as a clue to explore relationships between personal memory and collective history. The novel skillfully connects personal experiences with Taiwan's historical changes.

**Intergenerational transmission of war trauma:**
The novel deeply explores how war trauma transmits across generations. Even after war ends, its effects remain deeply imprinted in people's memories.

**Object memory:**
Wu explores how objects carry memory through a bicycle. Every object has its own story and serves as historical witness.

**Multiple time-space intersections:**
The novel uses multiple time-space intersection narrative techniques. Past and present, reality and fiction skillfully interweave in the story.

### International Response and Influence

**Translation quality importance:**
"The Stolen Bicycle's" English translation quality was excellent, allowing foreign readers to fully experience the original work's charm. Excellent translation is crucial for work internationalization.

**Literary criticism affirmation:**
Foreign literary critics gave this novel high praise. They appreciated Wu's unique narrative style and profound thematic content.

**Publishing market response:**
After overseas publication, the novel received good market response. Many bookstores listed it as recommended reading.

**Academic attention:**
Foreign academic circles began paying attention to Wu's creation. Many universities listed his works as research subjects.

## Teaching and Literary Promotion

### Teaching Work at National Dong Hwa University

**Department of Chinese Literature contributions:**
Wu currently serves as professor in National Dong Hwa University's Department of Chinese Literature, making important contributions to Taiwan's literary education in this position.

**Creative course design:**
He designed multiple literary creation courses, cultivating many young writers. His teaching style emphasizes creativity and personal style development.

**Cross-disciplinary teaching practice:**
Wu practices cross-disciplinary concepts in teaching, encouraging students to combine knowledge from different disciplines for creation.

**Academic research balance:**
He successfully balances relationships between creation and academic research, serving as both creator and scholar.

### Literary Award Jury Work

**Participation in major literary awards:**
Wu frequently serves as judge for various literary awards, contributing professional opinions to Taiwan literature's development.

**Discovering new writers:**
Through jury work, he helps discover and encourage new writers, contributing to Taiwan literature's generational continuity.

## Ecological Philosophy and Creative Concepts

### Nature Writing Theory

**Literature and ecology integration:**
Wu advocates integrating literature with ecological concepts. He believes literature can awaken people's environmental consciousness and promote ecological protection.

**Scientific precision and literary beauty:**
His creative philosophy emphasizes combining scientific precision with literary beauty. He believes good nature writing requires both scientific foundation and literary artistry.

**Cross-species perspective:**
Wu attempts to observe the world from cross-species perspectives. He believes humans need to transcend anthropocentric viewpoints and consider other species' positions.

### Future Development Direction

**Climate change literature:**
Facing increasingly serious climate change problems, Wu begins paying more attention to climate change literature, attempting to explore this theme through creative work.

**International cooperation expansion:**
He actively participates in international literary exchange activities, hoping to promote Taiwan literature through international cooperation.

**New generation cultivation:**
As educator and writer, Wu focuses on cultivating new generation creators, hoping to pass on nature writing traditions.

Wu Ming-yi represents contemporary Taiwan literature's unique voice. He successfully combines scientific knowledge with literary creation, opening new possibilities for nature writing. His works not only possess high literary value but carry important ecological and environmental significance. In an era of increasingly serious global environmental problems, Wu's literature provides important resources for contemplation and action. His international success also proves Taiwan literature's unique charm and value, contributing important strength to Taiwan's cultural soft power.
